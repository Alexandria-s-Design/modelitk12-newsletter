## What "Validated Model" Actually Means (And Why It Matters)

You've probably seen simulations where you click buttons and stuff happens.

**Cell Collective is different.**

Every public model in the library goes through a **rigorous validation process**:

### Step 1: Literature Curation

Researchers read **dozens to hundreds** of scientific papers about the system.

They extract every interaction:
- "Protein A activates Protein B (Smith et al., 2018)"
- "Gene X represses Gene Y (Johnson et al., 2020)"

**Every arrow in the network diagram has a citation.**

You can click on a connection and see: "This interaction is based on [these 3 studies]."

### Step 2: Model Construction

The interactions are encoded as **logical rules**:
- "B is ON when A is ON and C is OFF"
- "X inhibits Y by blocking its activation"

These rules capture the **essence** of the biological mechanism without requiring exact kinetic parameters.

### Step 3: Behavior Expectations

Researchers define what the model **should** do:
- "If we knock out Gene X, Protein Y should decrease"
- "If we activate Receptor Z, Pathway W should turn on"

These are test cases based on **known biology** from experiments.

### Step 4: Perturbation Testing

The model is tested with **simulated knockouts, overexpression, and inhibitors**.

If the model predicts outcomes that match published experimental results, it passes.

If not, **back to Step 2**.

### Step 5: Peer Review

Results are submitted to journals. External experts review the model, the logic, the validation.

**Only after passing peer review** does the model get published‚Äîand added to the Cell Collective library.

### Step 6: Public Release with Documentation

The validated model, all its logic rules, and all its references are **publicly available** in Cell Collective.

You can see:
- Every node and what it represents
- Every connection and its source
- Every rule and its logic
- Version history if it's been updated

**This is transparent science.**

- **Predictive power**: Knockouts and perturbations produce the same cause-and-effect outcomes

**Bottom line**: The **science** is authentic. The **experience** is age-appropriate.

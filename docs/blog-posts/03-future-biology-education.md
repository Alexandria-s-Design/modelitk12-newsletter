## The Research Revolution Happening Right Now

**Biology is undergoing the same transformation physics had in the 20th century**: Moving from descriptive science to predictive, computational, mathematical science.

### The Old Biology (Pre-2000s)

**Approach**: Study one gene, one protein, one pathway at a time

**Questions**: "What does this protein do?"

**Methods**: Knockout experiments, biochemical assays, microscopy

**Limits**: Can't understand complex systems with many interacting parts

### The New Biology (2000s-Present)

**Approach**: Study networks, systems, interactions across scales

**Questions**: "How do thousands of components work together to create behavior?"

**Methods**: Computational modeling, network analysis, systems biology

**Power**: Can predict emergent behaviors, design interventions, understand disease

## How to Keep the Momentum Going (Your Year 2 Playbook)

**You've spent 10 weeks learning about computational modeling.**

**Now what?**

**Here's the roadmap for embedding this into your teaching practice permanently:**

### Quarter 1: Start Small (1-2 Models)

**Introduce modeling with simple systems**:
- Gene regulation (lac operon)
- Enzyme feedback (product inhibition)
- Basic homeostasis (glucose-insulin)

**Goal**: Students get comfortable with the interface and workflow

**Time investment**: 2-3 class periods total

### Quarter 2: Build Complexity (3-5 Models)

**Move to multi-component systems**:
- Cell cycle control
- Signaling pathways
- Metabolic networks

**Goal**: Students handle more nodes, feedback loops, and perturbations

**Time investment**: 1 modeling activity every 2-3 weeks

### Quarter 3: Collaborative Projects (2-3 Team Models)

**Implement group modeling**:
- Cellular respiration (4 teams, 4 subsystems)
- Immune response (teams model different pathways)
- Ecosystem dynamics (abiotic/biotic factors)

**Goal**: Students practice collaboration, integration, peer review

**Time investment**: One major project per unit

### Quarter 4: Student-Designed Investigations (Independent Modeling)

**Let students drive**:
- They choose a biological question
- They design a model to investigate it
- They test hypotheses and present findings

**Goal**: Students become independent computational biologists

**Time investment**: Final project (2-3 weeks)
